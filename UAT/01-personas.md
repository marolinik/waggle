# UAT Personas

Eight personas representing Waggle's target market. Each has distinct needs, workflows, and emotional expectations.

---

## Persona 1: Mia — Solo Knowledge Worker

**Role**: Freelance consultant, strategy & research
**Tech level**: Comfortable with tools, not a developer
**Daily tools**: Notion, Google Docs, email, ChatGPT
**Pain point**: Juggles 4-6 client projects. Loses context between sessions. Spends 30 min every Monday rebuilding mental model of where each project stands.
**What she needs from Waggle**: "Open a workspace → instantly know where I left off → do real work → close it knowing nothing is lost"
**Success metric**: Time from app open to productive work < 30 seconds
**Emotional priority**: Orientation, Relief, Continuity

---

## Persona 2: Luka — Project Manager

**Role**: PM at a 50-person tech company, manages 3 concurrent projects
**Tech level**: Uses project tools daily, light technical knowledge
**Daily tools**: Linear, Slack, Google Meet, Confluence
**Pain point**: Status updates consume 2 hours/day. Team sync meetings are repetitive. Context switching between projects causes mistakes.
**What he needs from Waggle**: "Ask the agent what happened on Project X today. Get a draft status update. Track decisions across sprints."
**Success metric**: Status update draft in < 2 minutes. Decision history available on demand.
**Emotional priority**: Momentum, Seriousness, Controlled Power

---

## Persona 3: Ana — Product Manager

**Role**: PM at a SaaS startup, owns roadmap and specs
**Tech level**: Can read code, doesn't write it daily
**Daily tools**: Linear, Figma, Slack, Notion, user research tools
**Pain point**: Research insights get buried. Feature specs reference old decisions that nobody can find. Context for "why we decided X" disappears.
**What she needs from Waggle**: "Synthesize user research into a spec. Remember why we made past decisions. Draft PRDs that reference real context."
**Success metric**: PRD draft that includes relevant historical context without manual digging.
**Emotional priority**: Trust, Alignment, Continuity

---

## Persona 4: Marko — Developer / Technical Lead

**Role**: Senior dev, leads a small team, writes code + reviews PRs
**Tech level**: Expert
**Daily tools**: VS Code, Terminal, GitHub, Slack, Claude Code
**Pain point**: Claude Code is powerful but stateless. Every session starts from scratch. No memory of architectural decisions, past debugging sessions, or team conventions.
**What he needs from Waggle**: "Agent that remembers our architecture, past bugs, team conventions. Drafts that build on accumulated context. Code review that knows our patterns."
**Success metric**: Agent provides contextually relevant suggestions without re-explaining the codebase.
**Emotional priority**: Controlled Power, Trust, Momentum
**Competitive bar**: Must be clearly better than raw Claude Code for project-level work.

---

## Persona 5: Sara — Marketing Manager

**Role**: Leads content marketing at a B2B company
**Tech level**: Non-technical, uses SaaS tools
**Daily tools**: HubSpot, Canva, Google Analytics, Slack, WordPress
**Pain point**: Content calendar is chaotic. Brand voice drifts across pieces. Competitive intelligence is scattered in random docs.
**What she needs from Waggle**: "Draft blog posts in our brand voice. Maintain a competitive intelligence workspace. Track content performance insights."
**Success metric**: Blog draft that matches brand voice and references past content decisions.
**Emotional priority**: Alignment, Relief, Seriousness

---

## Persona 6: David — HR Manager

**Role**: HR lead at a growing company (80→150 people)
**Tech level**: Basic, uses HR platforms
**Daily tools**: BambooHR, Slack, Google Docs, email
**Pain point**: Onboarding documentation is always outdated. Policy questions get answered differently by different people. Exit interview insights get lost.
**What he needs from Waggle**: "Onboarding checklist that adapts. Policy knowledge base. Interview question generator. Track organizational insights."
**Success metric**: Onboarding checklist generated in < 5 minutes. Policy answers consistent with company docs.
**Emotional priority**: Seriousness, Trust, Relief

---

## Persona 7: Elena — Data Analyst

**Role**: Business analyst, SQL + dashboards + executive reports
**Tech level**: Technical (SQL, Python basics), not a software engineer
**Daily tools**: SQL client, Tableau/Looker, Excel, Slack, Jupyter
**Pain point**: Repetitive report generation. Explaining the same metrics to different stakeholders. Ad-hoc analysis requests with no context on why.
**What she needs from Waggle**: "Draft reports from data context. Remember metric definitions. Generate SQL templates. Track analysis history."
**Success metric**: Report draft that includes correct metric definitions without re-specifying them.
**Emotional priority**: Momentum, Trust, Controlled Power

---

## Persona 8: Team Lead — Cross-Functional Coordinator

**Role**: Leads a cross-functional team (design, eng, PM, QA) on a product initiative
**Tech level**: Mixed — coordinates between technical and non-technical people
**Daily tools**: Slack, Linear, Google Docs, Figma, various team tools
**Pain point**: Information fragmented across tools. Team alignment meetings are expensive. Nobody has the full picture.
**What they need from Waggle**: "Shared workspace where the whole team context lives. Agent that can answer 'where are we on X?' for any team member. Shared task board with agent-assisted progress tracking."
**Success metric**: Any team member can ask the agent about project status and get a coherent, current answer.
**Emotional priority**: Orientation, Momentum, Continuity
**Tier focus**: TEAMS
